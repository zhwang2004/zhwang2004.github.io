About Me

I am a senior undergraduate student at Nanjing University of Information Science and Technology, specializing in Internet of Things through an international collaborative program. My research focuses on machine learning algorithms with healthcare applications, particularly ensemble methods for cardiovascular disease diagnosis.

My work has resulted in multiple publications in respected scientific journals and a nationally authorized patent. I have developed novel approaches for enhancing the robustness and accuracy of diagnostic algorithms through adaptive techniques and parameter optimization.

I am currently seeking Ph.D. and research-oriented Master's opportunities to further develop my expertise in machine learning for healthcare and intelligent systems. I hope to join a research team where I can continue contributing to this field through innovative computational methods.

Publications

An Adaptive Weighted Boosting Framework for Enhanced Cardiovascular Disease Diagnosis

Wang, Z., Shao, Z., Wang, B., Cheng, X. (2025). An adaptive weighted boosting framework for enhanced cardiovascular disease diagnosis. Biomedical Signal Processing and Control, Elsevier. (SCI, JCR-Q1)
First Author Published in Biomedical Signal Processing and Control

Developed an innovative adaptive weighting mechanism for boosting algorithms that significantly improves cardiovascular disease diagnostic accuracy compared to traditional methods. The framework demonstrates enhanced robustness across diverse patient populations and clinical scenarios.

APPBoost: An Adaptive Parameter-Pair Boosting Algorithm for Enhanced Robustness Against Noise and Imbalance

Wang, Z., Shao, Z., Wang, B., Cheng, X. (2025). APPBoost: an adaptive parameter pair boosting algorithm for enhanced robustness against noise and imbalance. The Journal of Supercomputing, Springer. (SCI, JCR-Q2)
First Author Accepted by The Journal of Supercomputing

This research introduces a novel dual-parameter adjustment mechanism for boosting algorithms that systematically enhances model resistance to interference and improves robustness. The proposed APPBoost algorithm adaptively adjusts two key parameters simultaneously, optimizing performance in the presence of noise and data imbalance.

An Adaptive Enhanced Method for Cardiovascular Disease Diagnosis Using Weight Parameter Adjustment

First Inventor Nationally Authorized Patent

This patent documents a novel method for cardiovascular disease diagnosis utilizing adaptive weight parameter adjustments to enhance diagnostic accuracy and system reliability. The invention provides a systematic approach to parameter optimization that significantly improves performance in clinical applications.

  • Design identity alignment and modality alignment loss functions to optimize feature relationships at the identity and distribution levels, improving model performance.
